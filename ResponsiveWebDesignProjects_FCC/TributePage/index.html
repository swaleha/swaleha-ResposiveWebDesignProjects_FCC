Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India and people's President

Here's a time line of Dr. Kalam's life:

  • 1931 - Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • 1954 - Graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, then affiliated with the University of Madras
  • 1955 - Moved to Madras to study Aerospace Engineering in Madras Institute of Technology
  • 1960 - Graduated from Madras Institute of Technology and joined as Scientist in the Aerospace Development Establishment of Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO)
  • 1965 - First to start work on expandable rocket project
  • 1969 - Was transferred to Indian Space Research Organization. received the government's approval and expanded the programme to include more engineers.
  • 1970 - Directed two projects "Project Devil" and "Project Valiant" to develop ballistic missiles from SLV programmes. India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) was directed by Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam.
  • 1980 - Directed Advanced Missile Programme
  • 1981 - Was awarded Padma Bhushan from Government of India
  • 1992 - Served as Chief Scientific Advisor to the Prime Minister and Secretary of DRDO. Served as Chief Coordinator in the test planning for Pokhran-II tests along with Dr R Chidambaram, the Director of the Department of Atomic Energy
  • 1997 - Was honored with Bharat Ratna by the President of India, it's the India's highest civilian honour
  • 1998 - Kalam developed a low cost coronary stent, named the "Kalam-Raju Stent", along with cardiologist Soma Raju
  • 1998 - Received Veer Savarkar Award from Government of India.
  • 2000 - Received Ramanujan Award from Alwars Research Centre, Chennai
  • 2002 - Became 11th president of the Republic of India
  • 2007 - Received Honorary Doctorate of Science and Technology from Carnegie Mellon University
  • 2007 - Received King Charles II Medal from Royal Society, UK[
  • 2008 - Received Doctor of Engineering (Honoris Causa) from Nanyang Technological University
  • 2009 - Received Hoover Medal from ASME Foundation, USA
  • 2011 - Became visiting professor at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, the Indian Institute of Management Ahmedabad, and the Indian Institute of Management Indore; an honorary fellow of Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore; chancellor of the Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology Thiruvananthapuram; professor of Aerospace Engineering at Anna University; and an adjunct at many other academic and research institutions across India
  • 2011 - Was Awarded IEEE Honorary Membership
  • 2012 - Launched a anti-corruption youth programme called the "What Can I Give Movement"
  • 2012 - Received Doctor of Laws (Honoris Causa) from Simon Fraser University
  • 2013 - Received Von Braun Award from National Space Society
  • 2014 - Received Doctor of Science from Edinburgh University, UK
  • 2015 - Died at the age of 83 while delivering lecture on "Creating a Livable Planet Earth" at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
